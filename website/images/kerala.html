Best Tourist Places In Kerala That Are Travellers Delight

Tucked in the lap of bounty nature, Kerala -the little paradise in the southern part of India is famous for the best tourist places, ecotourism, Ayurvedic treatments, aromatic spices, verdant tea gardens, paddy fields, serene backwaters, tranquil beaches, stunning art forms, amazing wildlife, beautiful temples, tasty food, warm people and whatnot. 

Right from the best tourist places in Kerala to uncountable things to do, Godâ€™s own country is a place that will take your heart.

8 Best Tourist Places To Visit In Kerala

  • Alleppey
  • Munnar
  • Thekkady
  • Kovalam
  • Thrissur
  • Wayanad

  • Kumarakom

1. Alleppey

One of the oldest planned towns in India, Alleppey is an utterly beautiful place in Kerala which is famous for surreal backwater experience, stunning beaches, and verdant paddy fields. The official name of Alleppey is Alappuzha and this scenic town is visited by a large number of foreign as well as local tourists to relax at various Ayurvedic and yoga centers. People can also indulge in thrilling activities at Alappuzha beach. 

Best things to do in Alleppey

  • Houseboat Cruising at Alleppey
  • Ayurvedic Treatments
  • Yoga and Meditation
  • Parasailing, Motorboat rides, etc
  • Village Tour
  • Food Tour

Best time to visit Alleppey

The ideal time to visit this charming town is anytime in winters that fall between October and March.

2. Munnar

Home to mountains, green belts, and three rivers -Mattupetti, Periavaru, and Nallathanni, Munnar is another gem for Kerala Tourism that is a delightful discovery for tourists. Munnar is famous for its typical valley experience that includes expansive lush green tea gardens, exotic species of flora and fauna, and the most captivating vistas. This quaint hill station of Kerala is definitely worth visiting as it is also a popular romantic escapade.

Best things to do in Munnar

  • Salim Ali Bird Sanctuary
  • Eravikulam National Park
  • Atukkad Waterfall
  • Shikara ride in Kundala
  • Tea Gardens
  • Camping and Trekking

Best time to visit Munnar

The ideal time for visiting Munnar is anytime between March to September when the weather stays pleasant. Avoid monsoon season as it heavily pours, restricting most of the activities during this period.

3. Thekkady

Boasting the largest tiger reserve of India, Thekkady is also famous for its exquisite wildlife experience which includes pachyderms and exotic flora and fauna species. Another thing that makes Thekkady popular is the cardamom plantation that covers the hills. Enjoy the most stunning views if you plan a road trip to Thekkady as the distance between Thekkady and Munnar is barely 122kms which takes around two and half hours by road. Donâ€™t miss out on this little haven in Kerala which is tucked away from cacophony.  

Best things to do in Thekkady

  • Wildlife Safari
  • Trekking & Rafting
  • Sail through Periyar Lake
  • Watch Mullaperiyar Dam

Best time to visit Thekkady

October to February and June to September are ideal months to visit Thekkady which is one the best places to visit in Kerala.

4. Kovalam

Just 25 km from Trivandrum lies a small coastal town Kovalam which is famous for shimmering crescent beaches that have shallow waters and low tidal waves. The beaches of Kovalam have gained popularity amongst honeymooners and foreign tourists as it is quieter at this side. Also, the shoreline is impeccably clean and ideal for sunbathing, swimming, and getting sunkissed. Enjoy herbal body toning massages in this best tourist place in Kerala.

Best things to do in Kovalam

  • Basking on the Palm-backed beaches
  • Swimming in shallow waters
  • Catamaran cruising
  • Visit Vizhinjam Cave Temple
  • Visit Sagarika Marine Research Aquarium

Best time to visit Kovalam

The temperature during the winter season is conducive. You can plan your trip to Kovalam somewhere between November to February.

5. Thrissur

The land of sacred sites and vibrant festivals, Thrissur is where you can witness the beautiful side of culturally exquisite Kerala. Formerly known as Trichur, the cultural capital of Kerala is famous for ancient temples, beautiful churches, mosques, and museums. Thrissur is the Gold capital of India that is famous for the production of gold and diamond jewelry. One of the major attractions in Thrissur is colorful festivals such as Thrissur Pooram festival, Onam festival that are celebrated with great enthusiasm and marked by a large number of people.

Best places to visit in Thrissur

  • Vadakkumnathan Kshetram Temple
  • Tomb of Shakthan Thampuran
  • Archaeology Museum
  • Athirapally Falls
  • Heritage Garden

Best time to visit Thrissur

Enjoy the electrifying vibe of Thrissur during the festive season which is April and May. You can also visit anytime between the winter months i.e. October to March.

6. Wayanad

Nestled amidst beautiful hills and forests, Wayanad is among the most scenic places in Kerala as it houses enchanting waterfalls, wildlife sanctuaries, beautiful caves, and amazing sites for bird watching. One of the noteworthy places to visit in Wayanad is the Edakkal caves where Stone Age carvings are well preserved. If you are looking for a tourist place in Kerala which is perfect for camping then Wayanad must be on your wishlist as it is also famous for amazing trekking trails and camp stays.

Best places to visit in Wayanad

  • Muthanga Wildlife Sanctuary
  • Thirunelli Temple
  • Banasura Sagar Dam
  • Chembra Peak
  • Kuruva Island
  • Pookode Lake

Best time to visit in Wayanad

The peak season for tourists in Wayanad is from October to May. You can plan your trip during that time as the weather is pleasing enough for sightseeing and excursion.

7. Kochi

One of the most popular port cities in Kerala, Kochi has been given the title the Queen of Arabian Sea. Also known as Cochin, it is the gateway of Kerala which offers a lot to tourists. From the tranquil beauty of nature to ferry rides and also, bathing with elephants, thereâ€™s a lot to enjoy in Cochin. Be it a day tour to Kodanad, watch the fascinating Kathakali performances, getting pampered with Ayurvedic treatments, enjoying go-karting, shopping, eating; you can spoil yourself in Kochi. 

Best places to visit in Kochi

  • Bishop House: The Indo-Portuguese museum
  • St. Francis Church: The most popular church in Kochi
  • Muziris: Take a tour to the spice hub of Kerala
  • Paradesi Synagogue: The oldest Synagogue
  • Kodanad: A small village where you can enjoy bathing with elephants
  • Princess Street: Buy souvenirs

Best time to visit Kochi

Wintertime, October to February are the best months to visit Cochin as the weather is cool and pleasant for exploring the cityscape.

8. Kumarakom

Hills, forests, rivers, plains, sea, ghats - Kumarakom has the richest topography. One of the best tourist places in Kerala, this place is the gemstone of Kerala. This little village on Vembanad Lake is another tourist place in Kerala which is famous for experiencing the serenity of backwaters. Kumarakom is adorned with verdant coconut groves and stunning vistas whilst houseboat cruising is something you shouldnâ€™t miss.

Best things to do in Kumarakom

  • Kumarakom Beach
  • Kumarakom Bird Sanctuary
  • Thirunakkara Mahadeva Temple
  • Aruvikkuzhi Waterfall
  • Bay Island Driftwood Museum
  • Pathiramanal Island
  • Ancient Thazhathangady Mosque

Best time to visit Kumarakom

September to March is a favorable time to visit Kumarakom as the weather will allow you to have fun even during the daytime.

Frequently Asked Questions about Best Places to Visit in Kerala

Q. What are some Kerala tourist places?
A. Some popular Kerala tourist places include Munnar, Alleppey, Wayanad, Thekkady, Kovalam, and Kochi.

Q. What is the best time to visit Kerala?
A. The best time to visit Kerala is from October to March, when the weather is pleasant and cool. However, some destinations like Munnar can be visited throughout the year.

Q. Are there any beaches in Kerala?
A. Yes, Kerala is home to some beautiful beaches, such as Kovalam, Varkala, and Marari Beach.

Q. Are there any national parks in Kerala?
A. Yes, Kerala is home to several national parks, including Periyar National Park, Silent Valley National Park, and Eravikulam National Park. These parks are home to a variety of wildlife, including tigers, elephants, and Nilgiri tahr.

Q: What are some good tourist places to visit with family in Kerala?
A: Some of the best tourist places to visit with family in Kerala are Munnar, Wayanad, Alleppey, Thekkady, and Kovalam. These destinations offer a mix of scenic beauty, adventure activities, and cultural experiences that are perfect for a family vacation.

Q: What are some popular tourist places in Kerala to visit with friends?
A: Kerala has many popular tourist places to visit with friends, such as Fort Kochi, Varkala, Bekal, Kozhikode, and Kumarakom. These destinations offer a vibrant atmosphere, scenic beauty, and a chance to explore the local culture and cuisine.

Q: What are some romantic places to visit in Kerala for a honeymoon?
A: Kerala is known for its beautiful backwaters, lush green landscapes, and serene beaches, making it an ideal destination for a honeymoon. Some popular honeymoon destinations in Kerala include Munnar, Alleppey, Kumarakom, Wayanad, and Thekkady.

Q: What are the best tourist places in Kerala to visit in summer?
A: Kerala is a popular summer destination due to its pleasant climate and natural beauty. Some of the best tourist places to visit in Kerala during summer include Munnar, Wayanad, Alleppey, Kovalam, and Vagamon. These destinations offer a respite from the heat and a chance to explore the beauty of Kerala.

Q: What are the best places to visit in Kerala in winter?
A: Kerala has a pleasant climate during winter, making it an ideal time to visit. Some of the best places to visit in Kerala during winter include Thekkady, Kumarakom, Kovalam, Alleppey, and Varkala. These destinations offer a mix of scenic beauty, adventure activities, and cultural experiences that are perfect for winter travel.
